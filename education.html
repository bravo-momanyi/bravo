My Education

My journey in education began at Kiomakondo D.O.K. Primary School, nestled right in my village. I worked hard and excelled in my KCPE, scoring an impressive 376 marks. This was a proud moment for both me and my family.

I was then admitted to one of the top schools in the country — Nyambaria High School. There, I committed myself to excellence in academics and discipline. After four years of determination, I emerged with an A– (74 points) in KCSE.

Fueled by my passion for design and creativity, I earned a place at Kenyatta University, where I am currently pursuing a degree in Architecture. This program challenges me to think critically, design with purpose, and create with heart.

My dream is to become a successful and impactful architect — one who transforms not just buildings, but communities. I want to blend art and engineering to solve real-world problems, especially in rural and developing areas.

I believe education is more than grades — it’s about building character, vision, and responsibility. I am grateful for every step I’ve taken, and every step ahead that will shape the architect I’m becoming.
